Section B (Short Answer) Answers:
1. Different people living together in peace without disagreement or fight.
2. Esau and Jacob, Abraham and Lot (or Philemon and Onesimus, Isaac and Abimelech).
3. Jonathan.
4. Forgive the person and live happily together.
5. Share their belongings with others OR protect one another when there is danger OR give help to the needy.

Section C (Theory) Answers:
1. Peaceful co-existence is vital for harmony and progress. The biblical examples (Esau & Jacob, Abraham & Lot) show that resolving conflicts and choosing peace prevents further strife, fosters reconciliation, and allows for happy living, aligning with God's desire for community peace.
2. Jesus taught that if someone offends us, we should forgive them and live happily together. This teaching promotes peaceful co-existence by encouraging the resolution of conflicts through forgiveness, preventing grudges, and fostering an environment of harmony rather than prolonged disagreement or fights.
3. Jonathan loved David as his own soul. He demonstrated this love by warning David of King Saul's plans to kill him and hiding him. Their relationship culminated in a covenant, showcasing a deep, protective, and selfless bond.
4. The moral responsibilities include considering those around us, showing hospitality, telling the truth, and being open-minded. Examples of expression include sharing belongings, protecting one another in danger, and helping the needy.
5. Pupils can apply these principles by resolving disagreements through forgiveness, avoiding fights, sharing belongings, protecting classmates from harm, and offering help to those in need. This fosters a supportive and harmonious learning environment, reflecting the teachings of Jesus and biblical examples.
