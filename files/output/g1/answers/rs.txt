2. Esau and Jacob, Philemon and Onesimus, Isaac and Abimelech, Abraham and Lot.
3. Jonathan saved David by telling him King Saul's plans to kill him and by hiding him in a place far away.

5. Jesus advised us that if somebody offends us, we should forgive the person and live happily together.

Section C (Theory) Answers:
1. Peaceful co-existence means different people living together in peace, without disagreement or fight. Jesus taught that if someone offends us, we should forgive them and live happily together, emphasizing harmony and reconciliation.
2. Jonathan loved David as his own soul. He demonstrated unselfish love by warning David about King Saul's plan to kill him and by hiding him from Saul. Their love was so strong that they even made a covenant, showing deep loyalty and self-sacrifice.
3. Three instances are: 1. Esau and Jacob reconciled after Jacob took Esau's blessings from their father. 2. Philemon and Onesimus resolved their past issues. 3. Abraham prevented a quarrel between himself and his nephew Lot over grazing land.
4. The moral responsibilities of showing unselfish love include considering those around us, showing hospitality, telling the truth, being open-minded, showing love to those in danger, and helping the needy. It emphasizes a caring and supportive attitude towards others.
5. Pupils can apply these principles by sharing their belongings with others, protecting one another when there is danger, and showing love to those who are in need. This fosters a supportive and harmonious environment among them.
