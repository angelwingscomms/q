Section C (Theory) Answers:
1. Forgiveness is central to Jesus' teaching on peaceful co-existence. It allows individuals to overcome offenses and live harmoniously, preventing prolonged conflict and fostering reconciliation.
2. Abraham prevented a quarrel with Lot over land, demonstrating proactive conflict resolution. Esau and Jacob reconciled after Jacob's deception, showing a willingness to forgive and restore brotherhood, both illustrating the importance of active effort to maintain peace.
3. Jonathan's love for David was unselfish as he risked his own safety and defied his father, King Saul, to protect David. He loved David "as his own soul" and made a covenant. This implies putting the other's well-being above personal gain or loyalty, fostering deep trust and commitment in relationships.
4. The text highlights showing love and hospitality, telling the truth, and being open-minded. It emphasizes considering those around us and actively helping the needy and those in danger, as well as sharing belongings and protecting each other.
5. Pupils can apply these principles by sharing resources, protecting classmates from harm or bullying, offering help to those in need, being truthful and open in interactions, and practicing forgiveness for minor disagreements to maintain a harmonious environment.
