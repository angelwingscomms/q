Section C (Theory) Answers:
1. Peaceful Co-existence means different people living together without disagreement or fight. It is important for harmony, progress, and well-being in a community, preventing conflict and fostering unity.
2. Jesus taught us to live peacefully with others. He advised forgiveness for offenses, promoting reconciliation and living happily together, which are foundational for peaceful co-existence.
3. Jonathan showed unselfish love by loving David as his own soul. He demonstrated this by warning David of King Saul's plans to kill him and hiding him, even though Saul was his own father. They also made a covenant.
4. Showing love and unselfishness is crucial for building strong, compassionate communities. It fosters trust, mutual support, and hospitality, leading to a more truthful and open-minded society.
5. Pupils can demonstrate love by sharing belongings, protecting one another from danger, and giving help to the needy. These actions promote a caring environment and teach empathy and generosity.
