Section B (Short Answer) Answers:
1. Different people living together in peace without any disagreement or fight.
2. We should forgive the person and live happily together.
3. Esau and Jacob; Abraham and Lot (or Philemon and Onesimus; Isaac and Abimelech).
4. He told David Saul's plans and hid him in a place far away from Saul's sight.
5. Share our belongings with others; Protect one another when there is danger.

Section C (Theory) Answers:
1. Peaceful co-existence ensures harmony and prevents conflict. The text defines it as living together without disagreement or fight. It highlights Jesus' teaching on forgiveness and provides biblical examples like Esau and Jacob, Isaac and Abimelech, and Abraham and Lot, all of whom sought to resolve or prevent conflict to maintain peace.
2. Showing unselfish love involves considering those around us, demonstrating hospitality, telling the truth, and being open-minded. It emphasizes putting others' well-being first, as exemplified by Jonathan's actions towards David. This love fosters positive relationships and a supportive community.
3. Jonathan and David's relationship is a prime example of unselfish love. Jonathan loved David 'as his own soul.' He demonstrated this by risking his own safety to warn David of King Saul's murderous intentions and by hiding him. Their love was so profound they made a covenant, indicating a deep, selfless bond.
4. The text suggests several ways: showing love to those in danger, giving help to the needy, sharing belongings with others, and protecting one another. These actions promote a caring and supportive environment, fostering unity and well-being within the community.
5. Jesus taught forgiveness for those who offend us, enabling peaceful co-existence. He also encouraged showing love and hospitality, telling the truth, and being open-minded to others. These teachings emphasize compassion, reconciliation, and selfless care, forming the foundation for both peaceful living and unselfish relationships.
