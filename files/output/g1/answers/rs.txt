3. To show love, consider those around us, tell them the truth, and be open-minded.

Section C (Theory) Answers:
1. Jesus taught that forgiving those who offend us is crucial for living happily and peacefully together. This prevents disagreement and fosters harmonious co-existence by removing barriers to relationships.
2. The text mentions Abraham preventing a quarrel between him and his nephew Lot. This shows that unselfish love, prioritizing peace and the relationship over potential disputes, can effectively resolve conflicts.
3. Jonathan demonstrated unselfish love by loving David as his own soul. He actively protected David from his father, King Saul, by revealing Saul's murderous plans and hiding him. This exemplifies self-sacrifice, loyalty, and active care for the well-being of another.
4. Individuals have a moral responsibility to show love, hospitality, and open-mindedness to others. This includes considering those around them, telling the truth, and being receptive, thereby fostering positive community interactions.
5. Sharing belongings promotes generosity and creates a sense of community. Protecting one another builds trust and reinforces collective responsibility. Both contribute to a more loving, supportive, and harmonious environment among pupils.
