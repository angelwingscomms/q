Section C (Theory) Answers:
1. A television allows us to watch cartoons and news. It has buttons for changing channels and the volume.
2. A radio passes audio sound programmes and messages to the public. It has an antenna for receiving sound messages, a knob for turning to different stations, and a speaker through which sound is passed.
3. A computer is an electronic device that processes data and gives information. It has a monitor where you see what has been entered from the keyboard and a system unit that connects all other devices.
4. A digital wristwatch is worn on the wrist and displays time in numbers (hour, minute, second). A mobile GSM phone is a small radio telephone used for long-distance communication, having a screen and keys for dialing. The watch is for time display, the phone for communication.
5. IT devices are used to pass information. Examples like televisions for news/entertainment, radios for audio messages, calculators for arithmetic, computers for data processing, and mobile phones for communication demonstrate their crucial role in facilitating information exchange and various daily tasks.
