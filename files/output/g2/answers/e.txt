Section C (Theory) Answers:
1. Determiners introduce a noun and provide information about quantity, possession, or specificity (e.g., 'few' in 'few books').
2. Adjectives modify or describe nouns, adding detail about their qualities. Example: 'The *red* car.'
3. Adverbs describe verbs: manner (how, e.g., 'slowly'), time (when, e.g., 'late'), place (where, e.g., 'here'). They clarify action circumstances.
4. Prepositions show the relationship between a noun/pronoun and other words, indicating location, time, or direction (e.g., 'The cat is *under* the table').
5. 'Is' is used with singular nouns/pronouns (e.g., 'He *is* happy'). 'Are' is used with plural nouns/pronouns (e.g., 'They *are* friends').
