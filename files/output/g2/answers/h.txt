Section C (Theory) Answers:
1. Professor Wole Soyinka was actively involved in Nigeria's political history and its struggle against British Colonization. He was also imprisoned for many years due to his activism, demonstrating his commitment to political change and human rights.
2. Chief Anthony Enahoro was a leading anti-colonial and pro-democracy activist. His most significant contribution was being the first Nigerian to move the motion for Nigeria's independence, which was eventually granted in 1960. This act was pivotal in the country's journey to self-governance.
3. Alhaja Kudirat Abiola fought for Nigeria to be free from military rule and for the establishment of a government of the people, by the people, and for the people. She stood by her husband, Chief MKO Abiola, after he won the 1993 presidential election. She was tragically killed in 1996 while her husband was in prison, a martyr for democracy.
4. A traditional ruler is a cultural leader responsible for protecting the traditions and culture of their people. Their title is recognized by the government of the state, signifying their official standing and influence within their ethnic unit or clan, even though their role is primarily cultural rather than political.
5. Traditional rulers serve as custodians of culture, preserving customs, languages, and heritage. They play a vital role in settling disputes, particularly land and marriage quarrels, contributing to community harmony. They support good leaders and educate their people on civic duties like voting and paying taxes. Furthermore, they enforce community norms by punishing bad behavior and ensuring people obey local laws and order, thereby maintaining social stability.
