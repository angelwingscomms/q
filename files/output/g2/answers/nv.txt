Section C (Theory) Answers:
1. Individuals consume substances for various reasons, including medicinal purposes (drugs keep people alive), satiety (food keeps hunger away), pleasure (soft drinks for enjoyment), social influence (seeing others consume them), and curiosity (desire to taste or experience effects).
2. Overdose refers to the act of consuming an excessive amount of food or drink. The signs of overdose include stooling, vomiting, fainting, sleeplessness, and stomach-pain. These symptoms indicate the body's adverse reaction to overwhelming intake.
3. Excessive consumption of substances has distinct consequences. Drinking too much leads to frequent urination, and in children, excessive water intake before bed can cause bed-wetting. Adults consuming too much alcohol become intoxicated. Inhaling too much smoke can lead to fainting.
4. When encountering someone suffering from an overdose, immediate action is crucial. The person should be taken to the hospital without delay. If the person faints, first aid should be applied to revive them. Additionally, any child observed smoking or drinking alcohol should be reported to their teacher and parents.
5. Food is essential for human well-being as it enables growth, sustains life, and provides necessary energy. Common examples of food items mentioned include bread, eggs, yam, garri, rice, beans, millet, mango, carrot, fish, meat, banana, and orange. Food sources in the community include farms and markets, with meat derived from animals like cows, goats, sheep, and hens.
