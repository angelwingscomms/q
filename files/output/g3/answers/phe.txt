Section C (Theory) Answers:
1. Rest is vital because it allows the body to relax muscles after prolonged work, preventing harm. It supports growth in younger individuals, revitalizes the mind and body, improves focus, and ensures the proper functioning of the brain and heart.
2. Ideal sleeping conditions include a comfortable and well-ventilated room, and clean, comfortable beddings. Avoiding overcrowding on the bed or in the room is also crucial. These conditions ensure physical comfort, adequate air circulation, hygiene, and personal space, all contributing to restorative sleep.
3. First aid is the immediate care provided to an injured or sick person before professional medical help arrives. Its primary objectives are to alleviate the victim's pain and to prevent their condition from deteriorating further while waiting for medical attention.
4. To stop severe bleeding, first calm the patient and have them lie flat. Reassure them to reduce fear. Loosen clothing around the neck and waist for easier breathing. Elevate the bleeding body part to reduce blood flow. Finally, raise the foot of the bed to direct more blood to the brain and prevent fainting.
5. Fainting is a temporary loss of consciousness, where an individual loses awareness of their surroundings. A potential cause mentioned in the text is an insufficient supply of air or blood to the brain.
