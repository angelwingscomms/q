Section C (Theory) Answers:
1. Rest is crucial as it allows the body to relax muscles after prolonged work, preventing harm. It aids growth in children and adolescents, refreshes mind and body, improves concentration, and ensures proper brain and heart function.
2. A conducive sleeping environment requires a comfortable and well-ventilated room. Beddings must be clean and comfortable. Additionally, it is important to avoid overcrowding of people on the same bed and in the same room to ensure a peaceful rest.
3. First Aid is defined as the initial assistance provided to an injured or sick individual before professional medical help arrives. Its primary objectives are to alleviate the victim's pain and to prevent their condition from deteriorating further.
4. To stop severe bleeding, first, calm the patient and have them lie flat. Second, reassure the patient to alleviate fear. Third, loosen any restrictive clothing around the neck and waist for easy breathing. Fourth, elevate the bleeding part of the body to reduce blood flow to the area. Finally, raise the foot of the bed to promote blood flow to the brain and prevent fainting.
5. Fainting is a temporary loss of consciousness where a person loses awareness of their surroundings. According to the text, it primarily occurs due to an insufficient supply of air or blood to the brain.
